after making sure everything will remain peaceful on the ship while you're gone, you jump ship onto the rocky land of the cave the ship had landed at. the crew allows you to move to the front, though you hardly feel you deserve the right. you have no idea where to go.

"so, have we been spelunking before or are we all going in blind?"

your question sends a ripple through the crowd and thomas steps forward, along with some other crewmembers you haven't had the chance to get to know yet. they gather around you, but kept the lead to you and thus begins your adventure into the cave.

the first few minutes you're walking, the crew chattering about the storm and potential repairs of the ship that will need to be done. as you go further into the cave and deeper down, the talking quiets. you can feel the crew becoming steadily uneasy and you're inclined to feel the same.

after months at sea, you're used to the sway of the water and the constant breeze that keeps the air flowing around you. having this stillness, this quiet, unnerves you. it should be a relief, but it isn't.

thomas expresses his unease within the cave and you agree with him.

"captain, i think we should take it easy. we have all been shaken by the storm and captain bradshaw."

a wet, squelching noise followed by a ear-piercing screech made your bones turn to liquid. the crew lurched and pushed you forward more in their attempt to escape whatever it may be that made that noise.

there is a fork in the path in front of you. you can still command the crew if you turn one way or the other. which way will you turn?
