if you squint down the tunnel, you can see the slightest blue color emanating from it. it puts you at ease, but not enough to follow it without caution completely. you draw your sword from its sheath. it's not the most impressive blade, having been stolen from the market just before you boarded captain bradshaw's ship. but it offers you some comfort, and the smallest sense of protection. the others behind you do the same and you're relieved more that the others don't find you to be a coward.

"we're going to the left, everyone. follow close and keep your eyes open for any danger."

that sickening squelch from before rears its head once more and the crew is eager to get moving if the pressure against your back is anything to go by. roberts is closest to you, and her fingers brush the sleeve of your shirt. her shuddering breaths cannot be disguised in the dark, but she soldiers on like everyone else. you suppose that if she had to be your first mate, you could not have chosen a better partner.

your calves burn as you adjust your walk to avoid slipping on the downward slope of the tunnel. some of the crew behind you scrapes against the rocks, but you know you'd be in the same position if you weren't at the front with everyone watching you. though you consider the crew to be your family, they are exceedingly tough on anyone and everyone. if you mess up, no mercy would be shown. it was like having fifteen or so siblings watch your every move.

as you continue to lead your fellow crew members through the tunnel, and as you do, the rock walls surrounding your party glow brighter and brighter blue. you studied before becoming a pirate, so you know this is most likely due to bioluminescence within the soil and the rocks themselves. some of the crew behind you murmured about magic and witches, which caused quite the stir among everyone else. pirates, as you have learned the past few months, are one of the most superstitious groups of people you've ever met.

"everyone," you call out, sounding more confident than you felt on the inside. roberts' hand on your arm was turning your legs to marmalade. "there is nothing to fear. if we stick together, anything that might come at us will be outnumbered.

pirate pride is a stubborn and solid thing, so the crew calms down. it's only a little while further that the tunnel widens again and you feel you can breathe easier. roberts lets go of your arm, and the absence of her touch leaves you shivering. in front of you, the mouth of the tunnel gapes hungrily and further along is more of the blue light, along with purple and green. it shimmers along the walls of the tunnel like the fish you'd seen in the tropical waters a mere few weeks ago. the crew behind you urges you forward with their eagerness.
